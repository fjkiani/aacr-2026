I think this audience is probably familiar with the cell cycle, but very briefly, it is the process by which a cell grows, duplicates DNA, and divides in two. And in a healthy cell, the orderly transition between the phases of the cell cycle is a tightly regulated process controlled by cyclin-dependent kinase phosphorylation events, and those are regulated even further by their cognate cyclins. The cyclin serves two key functions here.
