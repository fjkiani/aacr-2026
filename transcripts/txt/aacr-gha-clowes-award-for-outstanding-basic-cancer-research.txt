He also led the first whole genome bisulfite sequencing analysis of human cancer, revealing increased methylation variability as a defining feature of colorectal cancer. Dr. Feinberg has also made seminal contributions to understanding cancer plasticity by integrating cancer biology with concepts from information theory and statistical physics. His work established epigenetic entropy as a defining feature of the cancer epigenome and showed that chromatin structure can deform this landscape in ways that promote variable gene expression and phenotypic plasticity. Importantly, Dr. Feinberg showed that metastasis in pancreatic cancer can be driven by epigenetic plasticity in the absence of metastasis-specific driver mutations, challenging long-standing models of cancer progression. He further identified linked metabolic and epigenetic programs that promote distant metastasis, revealing new mechanistic insights into tumor progression and potential therapeutic vulnerabilities.
