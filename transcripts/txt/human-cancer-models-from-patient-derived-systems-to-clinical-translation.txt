So precision oncology has fundamentally changed how we classify cancer. Through genomic and molecular profiling, we can now identify actionable alterations with unprecedented resolution, and yet a critical gap remains. Tumors with similar genomic profiles often behave very differently in patients. They respond differently to therapy. They evolve under therapy and under therapy pressures. And so they develop resistance through mechanisms that still we probably don't fully understand, and that are not always predictable with genome sequencing alone. So this session actually is designed to discuss the translational relevance of human tumor systems, bridging discovery and validation with their clinical implementation to advance human cancer treatment. This symposium is built around a simple but consequential premise. To truly personalize cancer therapy, we must move beyond inference and toward direct measurements of therapeutic response in human tumor models. In other words, we must not only understand and ask what the tumor is, but what the tumor does when challenged with therapy.
