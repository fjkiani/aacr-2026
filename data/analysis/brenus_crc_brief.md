## 2. Pivotal Talks for STC-1010

### Luis Diaz Jr. (MSK) — MMR Deficiency Induction
> *"Temozolomide and cisplatin induce a high increase in TMB and MSI score... patients with high TMB gain and MSI gain survived longer"*

- **Trial:** Temozolomide + cisplatin + PD-1 in MSS CRC — **missed primary endpoint**
- **STC-1010 relevance:** Validates MSS→MSI immunogenicity conversion concept. STC-1010 hapten approach may achieve equivalent immunogenic priming without chemotherapy toxicity burden.
- **Biomarker gap:** No baseline MSH2 methylation predictor; no serial ctDNA MSI tracking. **CrisPRO opportunity:** model conversion-likely patients for STC-1010 selection.

### Scott Kopetz (MD Anderson) — RAS/RAF in MSS mCRC (BreakWater context)
> *"Those with high cell-cycle signaling at baseline are much more likely to have secondary mutations"*

- **Key finding:** YAP/TEAD activation is near-universal resistance mechanism after KRAS/RAF inhibition in MSS CRC
- **STC-1010 relevance:** mFOLFOX6 backbone context — chemotherapy resistance landscape informs combination sequencing. KRAS/RAF-resistant patients may be ideal STC-1010 candidates.
- **CrisPRO opportunity:** Baseline transcriptomic risk score for adaptive mutability → patient selection.

### Ignacio Melero (CIMA/Navarra) — FAP-4-1BB in MSS CRC
> *"FAP-targeted 4-1BB ligand trimer amplifies T-cell engager in MSS colorectal cancer"*

- **Key finding:** Conditional FAP-targeting avoids systemic 4-1BB toxicity; amplifies T-cell response in MSS CRC TME
- **STC-1010 relevance:** Direct combination partner opportunity — 4-1BB costimulation + hapten-mediated T-cell priming
- **Stage:** Phase 1/2 (cevisatuximab + 4-1BBL trimer)

### Junyoung Wang — NAT10-MYC Loop / MHC-I Loss
> *"NAT10-MYC loop drives autophagy-dependent MHC-I downregulation and immunotherapy resistance in CRC"*

- **STC-1010 relevance:** MHC-I loss is a key resistance mechanism to T-cell-based immunotherapy. Hapten-mediated killing may bypass MHC-I requirement — potential differentiation claim.
